Steamed Salmon

Description

A quick and easy salmon recipe that never disappoints.

Whether as a weekday meal for just yourself, or a big dinner party, you'll have this melt-in-your-mouth salmon on the table within 30min.

Highly customizable - feel free to use any of the listed optional additions.

Ingredients:

  • Salmon filet(s), with or without skin, any size
  • Olive (or other) oil
  • Salt
  • Optional: black pepper
  • Optional: butter
  • Optional: garlic cloves, peeled and smashed
  • Optional: any fresh herbs (like thyme or rosemary)
  • Optional: lemon slices
  • Optional: vegetables (broccoli, asparagus, carrots...)

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 180 C.
  2. Generously season salmon with salt (and black pepper, if using) on each side.
  3. Get a piece of tin foil, large enough to place the salmon and fold over it.
  4. Place the tin foil on a baking sheet, partially hanging out and pour a bit of oil on the part that's on the baking sheet.
  5. Place salmon on the oiled foil top-side down, then turn it over on the bottom side (this way both sides are thinly covered in oil).
  6. If using garlic, place a clove on the highest point of each salmon filet.
  7. If using butter, place a small knob on top of the garlic.
  8. If using fresh herbs, place a sprig on top or the side of the salmon filets
  9. If using lemon slices, place one on top of each salmon filet.
  10. If using vegetables, chop them up and place around the salmon filets, making sure you have 1-2cm of free tin foil left on each side.
  11. Fold the remaining tin foil over the salmon and then fold all the edges together to create a sealed pocket.
  12. Place in the oven and bake for 15-30min depending on the size of your filets. (15 for single portion filets, 30 for half a fish)
