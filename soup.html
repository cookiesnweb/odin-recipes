Cream of Any Vegetable Soup

Description

More of a guide than a recipe, with which you can make a creamy soup of any vegetable you have/want.

Easily customizable, you can do anything from a healthy vegan soup all the way to a fat-filled, bacon-topped calorie bomb.

Ingredients:

Category 1: Flavor enhancers

  • 1-2 Onions of any kind
  • 2-5 Garlic cloves
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Other spices (powdered paprika, coriander, turmeric, cumin, etc)
  • 2-5 Bay leaves
  • 1-2 sprigs of any herb

Category 2: Base vegetables

  • Potatoes (highly recommended for bulking up vegetables with high water content, like mushrooms, zucchini, spinach, peppers)
  • For a "white" soup: cauliflower, mushrooms, celery root
  • For a "green" soup: broccoli, zucchini, spinach, peas, celery stalks
  • For a "red" soup: tomatoes, peppers, pumpkin, carrots

Category 3 (optional): Toppings

  • Croutons: a couple of slices of any bread
  • Dairy: milk, heavy cream, sour cream, feta cheese, cheddar cheese, any cheese
  • Meat: bacon bits, sausage
  • Greens: parsley, dill, basil, chives, green onions
  • Misc: pumkin seeds, sunflower seeds, chickpeas, walnuts, almonds, sun dried tomatoes

Steps:

  1. Choose your ingredients, in any combination, from each category.
  2. Chop all the vegetables in big chunks, enough of them to fill around 1/2 of the pot you want to use.
  3. Heat up enough oil to cover the bottom of your pot.
  4. If using onions and garlic, toss them in the oil and cook until fragrant.
  5. Add all your chopped vegetables and season generously with salt, pepper and any other spices you may be using.
  6. Mix well, so the vegetables are covered with everything else in the pot.
  7. Pour enough water to cover your vegetables and throw in the bay leaves and fresh herbs, if using.
  8. Cover the pot and turn the heat up until the water starts simmering; then turn the heat down to keep the simmer.
  9. Now walk away for 20 min.
  10. Check if the vegetables are cooked by cutting one in half - it should be easy to do so even with a spoon.
  11. Turn the stove off and take the bay leaves and herb sprigs out.
  12. Pur√©e the soup with a stab mixer until smooth. If using milk or heavy cream, add it now and then mix some more until incorporated.
  13. Taste and adjust seasoning.

Prepare optional toppings:

  • Croutons: cut the bread slices into cubes, toss with a bit of oil. Bake at 160 C for 15 min.
  • Bacon/sausage bits: cook in a pan until browned on all sides.
  • Seeds or nuts: toast in a pan until fragrant or bake at 160 C for 15 min.
  • Herbs: chop finely and sprinkle in each bowl.
  • Dairy: add about a spoonful of grated/crumbled cheese or sour cream in each bowl.
